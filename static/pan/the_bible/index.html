The Bible

Many people (including me, for many years) believe that the Bible is an accurate historical document. Because the Bible itself claims to be inspired (2 Tim 3:16), that adds weight to that idea. If God was the author, of course it's accurate.

When the evidence is actually considered, it's quite clear that it's not. Almost at all.

Let's consider just a few of the highlights in the history that the bible presents, then consider their accuracy.

  • Creation: God creates the universe, the earth, and all life.
  • The Garden: Adam and Eve, the first humans, commit the first sin.
  • The Flood: Regretting his mistakes, God wipes out all life on the planet, except for a smallish collection of animals and 8 humans.
  • The Patriarchs: Abraham is chosen by God to be the father of his chosen people, and given promises.
  • Egyptian Slavery: Having grown large, the family moves to Egypt, and ends up becoming enslaved then dramatically escaping.
  • The Conquest: Having left Egypt, this people violently takes over new territory, the "promised land."
  • David and Solomon: Having painfully switched from theocracy to monarchy, God blesses some good and incredibly powerful kings.
  • Jesus: The most vital figure in Christian Theology.
    • He was born
    • He died
    • He was raised

These are only a small collection of the events portrayed in the Bible, but they are also disproportionately important. And when there turns out to be substantial evidence that their accounts are, at best, wildly inaccurate -- this gives us no basis for choosing to believe the claims of the religions built upon these accounts.
