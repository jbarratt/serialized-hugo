About this site

I grew up in a very God-believing home and community, and that's no longer the case. I wrote what is probably too much about that in the 'About Me' page.

After years of study, thought, and conversation, my views have changed quite a lot.

My hope is that this site can provide a bridge for those who, finding themselves in a mindset similar to the one I started in, would like to understand more of the path I've taken; would like to understand more about the views I now hold and the reasons I hold them.

I understand there are many people who aren't interested in crossing the bridge. Even looking at the bridge might be unpleasant. That's ok, the bridge isn't for you. This is an opt-in bridge.

Since I came out as an atheist, I've been involved in a lot of conversations (face to face, phone, electronic mail, etc) with friends, family, and the faithful. At this point, many of these conversations are covering ground that I've covered at some length in the past; and yet, the conversations keep happening. I'm happy for them to happen; they're often a sign of love on the part of the converser. But they often require "building the bridge" by hand before we can get to any substantial dialog.

So, one reason for making this site is sheer laziness. I can direct people here, where my evidence and reasoning, as flawed as it certainly is, is on display. "Here is my bridge. I am delighted to have you inspect it."

The potential that my facts are suspect or reasoning is flawed is another reason to publish all of this. Given the choice to be 'correct' or 'incorrect', I'll choose 'correct.' I've changed a lot to get here, and I'll be really disappointed if I don't get to learn I've been wrong about more things in the future. So, it's a place to trap errors, missing evidence and logical fallacies.

The third big reason is that I've been exploring the 'big questions' -- how did we get here, where might we be going, what's the explanation for all this mess -- for a while now, so this is also functioning as a kind of journal of discovery.

Content

Because of my context, much of this site will be addressing two core questions:

  • Is the Bible true? Is it a reliable thing to build a life around?
  • Outside of the bible, is there any evidence to suggest that a God or Gods exist?

At present, I find the answer to both of those to be 'very probably not.'

The remainder of the site will be other related, but hopefully more positive, explorations.

Structure

As this site was mostly spawned from discussions I've had, it felt most natural to make it discussion-esqe. That's meant lots of links to other, related articles, where I discuss things, and occasional snippets of imaginary conversation:

You mean like this?

That's it, exactly. Having spent many years as a believer, I can sometimes anticipate the way (at least I) may have responded to a given point in the past -- and, having had many conversations around these topics, they're often based on real responses I've gotten.

What if you didn't anticipate my question/objection/comment, smartypants?

Awesome, please share it with me! If you know me already, feel free to send an email, otherwise I'm working on a feedback mechanism to provide a bit of a buffer from the complete unfiltered internet.

Apparent Certainty

I am not 100% certain about a single word I have written on this site.

However, if what I want to say is

The sky is blue today

It gets a little hard to read if I'm always writing all the qualifiers.

Because I have a lot of prior evidence that my ability to sense the light wavelengths which correspond to the color blue is accurate and intact, and that I have a lot of prior evidence for blue skies in the past, and a to-date coherent theory about the mechanisms that lead to the sky appearing to be blue in the first place, it's extremely probable that the sky does appear blue at this moment.

So be aware that they're there in my head. If I write something in a certain-y kind of way, that means I find it extremely probable. If I think it's very probable, or somewhat probable, or somewhat improbable, I'll usually say something like that.

What's with the name?

As I discuss a lot, I think probabilities are about the only useful way to try and come to grips with the world. And, as far as I've been able to discover, it's the most probable explanation (of all the theories I've yet explored) is that this world, the origins of life, the origins of the universe -- are all natural. Not Supernatural. Not a product of any one of the thousands of versions of God that humanity have believed in over the ages. But, I'm not sure, and I don't think anyone can be sure.

So, it's "Probably All Natural."
