I had a conversation with a believer once (when I was heavily questioning my own belief) which has stuck with me, and relates to the content of this site. On the topic of some books (on historical analysis of the origins of the Bible) he said:

But aren't you worried that these books are dangerous and could be harmful to people's faith?

Yes, in exactly the way that any pertinent evidence is "dangerous" to any idea which is not true.

It comes down to what your goal is.

If you want to preserve your belief at any cost, then yes, you should protect yourself from information that might challenge those beliefs.

If, on the other hand, your beliefs about the absolute truth of the universe are absolutely true, then there should be no such thing as "dangerous" information.

So if you are willing to call any information "dangerous", there's a good chance that your beliefs are not "true."
