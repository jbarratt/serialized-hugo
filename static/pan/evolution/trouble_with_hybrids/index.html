I am often told by the more educated people who wish to discuss this topic that they actually accept Evolution -- that they have been forced to, by the overwhelming evidence for it.

Sometimes it's suggested that Gen 1:2 allows for many years to pass, and thus for lots of things to happen in that gap (the solution which soothed me for many years) -- but this has serious problems, because the rest of the Genesis 1 narrative contains the creation of sun, stars, moon, and the rest of the creatures; sunless evolution would be a trick. Also, even if Genesis 1 didn't become internally inconsistent when interpreted that way, by the time we get to the flood story, there are insurmountable problems.

The idea that somehow Evolution and the Bible as Truth can co-exist is one fronted by many prominent Christians, as well. Dinesh d'Souza, for example, suggests that though life evolved on earth, that's the easy part -- "just the software" -- and God built the "computer" that this runs on. Creation by way of (God-assisted) Evolution.

I discuss some of the issues with this stance when addressing the idea that the Bible is a 'Why', not a 'How' book.. Because Christian faith is dependent on the belief that at least some things are true and have impact in the real world, it must be anchored in some kind of truth at some point -- if we are to believe that there is power to actually impact our present, and eternal future.
