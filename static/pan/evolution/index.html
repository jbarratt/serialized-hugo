Evolution

Addressing the question of "does the bible tell an accurate story of how we got here", there are three basic approaches I've been on the receiving end of.

  1. The bible is meant to be a "why" book, not a "how" book. The evidence we see around us doesn't matter.
  2. Evolution is invalid, and does not disagree with the biblical record, because it is false.
  3. A hybrid model, in which evolution is allowed to have happened, and somehow Genesis is to be understood as a metaphor or parable.

Option (2) seems to be the most popular.

A plea for a moment of self-honesty

If you are reading this and believe evolution is false, please take a good hard look at what your motives were when you went and explored the topic. Did you really want to understand evolution? Or did you want to feel like, though your beliefs conflict with evolution, that you could comfortably continue holding them?

I talk more about this: How much do you really want to understand?

The best book I've found so far for getting a good introduction to Evolution, and the mountains of evidence for it, is "Why Evolution is True". If you haven't read it, please open your mind and check it out. Even if you don't accept it's conclusions, it will at least help you be more informed about what it is you may disagree with.

Why Evolution Is True

Against my better judgement

  • I don't think trying to disprove evolution is the right place to start.
  • I also prefer not to discuss the topic with people who have only studied it enough to be able to prove a straw man version of it wrong.
  • I'm also not the best person to learn about it from, because I'm a curious amateur. Please read an actual expert-written, non-Christian book on the topic.

... but, it comes up so often that I do want to address some of the common issues that are raised. This will be by no means a comprehensive proof of evolution.

Some common objections

I don't believe in Evolution because of (some technical reason, perhaps based on mutation rates or information theory)

Most of the time this has been raised, it doesn't come with a better explanation for the mountains of evidence we're surrounded by for evolution. I talk about this a bit more in the Dead Body Problem.

Science has a well-known secular bias. You can't pay attention to anything they say, they're just promoting an atheistic agenda.

If I were feeling flip, I'd suggest an alternative explanation, which is that science has a well-known reality bias, but it's a common enough objection that it deserves a more considered response.

Science only works when you can do experiments and reproduce things. Science should have nothing to say about how evolution happened, because it can't be reproduced.

Another suprisingly common assertion, which I address in Timely Science.
