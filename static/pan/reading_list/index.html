Why I Believed: Reflections of a Former Missionary I really liked this book (so much that a blurb from me appears on the author's website) because it parallels a lot of my own experience -- the slowly dawning realization that you might be signed up for the wrong religion, while still loving many people who still want to be in it.

The Bible Unearthed: Archaeology's New Vision of Ancient Israel and the Origin of Its Sacred Texts I grew up with the idea that the findings of Archaeology are consistent with and reinforce the Bible's version of events. This provides a very alternative view (and a coherent story for why and how the Old Testament was written.)

Why Evolution Is True The best intro to Evolution and the vast evidence for it I have found yet.

Daylight Atheism Does a good job addressing a lot of the more logical and philosophical problems with God belief.

A Guide to the Good Life: The Ancient Art of Stoic Joy I put this on the list because I found it very helpful in dealing with some of the painful process of "deconversion."

Proving History: Bayes's Theorem and the Quest for the Historical Jesus The most geeky book on the list; brings a powerful logical tool to bear to think about what we can know (and how certain we can be) about the past -- and how to express that numerically.
