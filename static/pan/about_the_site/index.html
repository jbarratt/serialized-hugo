About this site

I grew up in a very God-believing home and community, and that's no longer the case. I wrote what is probably too much about that in the 'About Me' page.

Since I came out as an atheist, I've been involved in a lot of conversations (face to face, phone, electronic mail, etc) with friends, family, and the faithful. At this point, many of these conversations are covering ground that I've covered at some length in the past; and yet, the conversations keep happening. So, one reason for making this site is sheer laziness. I can direct people here, where my evidence and reasoning, as flawed as it certainly is, is on display.

The potential that my reasoning is flawed is another reason to publish all of this. Given the choice to be 'correct' or 'incorrect', I'll choose 'correct.' I've changed a lot to get here, and I'll be really disappointed if I don't get to learn I've been wrong about more things in the future. So, it's a place to trap errors, missing evidence and logical fallacies.

The third big reason is that I've been exploring the 'big questions' -- how did we get here, where might we be going, what's the explanation for all this mess -- for a while now, so this is also functioning as a kind of journal of discovery.

What's with the name?

As I discuss a lot, I think probabilities are about the only useful way to try and come to grips with the world. And, as far as I've been able to discover, it's the most probable explanation (of all the theories I've yet explored) that this world, the origins of live, the origins of the universe -- are all natural. Not Supernatural. Not any one of the thousands of versions of God that humanity have believed in over the ages. But, I'm not sure, and I don't think anyone can be sure.

So, it's "Probably All Natural."
